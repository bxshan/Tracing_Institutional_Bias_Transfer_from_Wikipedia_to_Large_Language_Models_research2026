TITLE: Kamehameha Schools
============================================================

[ORG], formerly called [ORG] ([ORG]), is a private school system in [ORG] established by [ORG], under the terms of the will of Princess [PERSON], who was a formal member of [ORG]. [ORG]'s will established a trust called [ORG]Bernice Pauahi Bishop Estate" that is [ORG]'s largest private landowner. Originally established in 1887 as an all-boys school for [NORP] children, it shared its grounds with [ORG]. The first graduating class consisted of 14 boys.

After it moved to another location, the museum took over two school halls. [ORG] opened the girls' school required by the will in 1894. It became coeducational in 1965. The 600-acre (2.4 km2) Kapālama campus opened in 1931, while the Maui and [ORG] campuses opened in 1996 and 2001, respectively.
It was developed at the bequest of Princess [PERSON] to educate the children of [GPE] with a fund set aside for orphaned and indigent children of [NORP] descent, and is designed to serve students from preschool through twelfth grade. The school teaches in the English language a college-prep education enhanced by [NORP] culture, language and practices, imparting historical and practical value of continuing [NORP] traditions. It operates 31 preschools statewide and three grade K–12 campuses in [GPE], [NORP], Maui, and [GPE], [GPE].
As the terms of its founding leaves the admission policy to the board of Trustees, the school has adopted an admissions policy fully preferential to applicants with [NORP] ancestry. Since 1965 it has excluded all but two [NORP] from being admitted. A lawsuit challenging the school's admission policy resulted in a narrow victory for [PERSON] in [ORG]; however, [PERSON] ultimately settled, paying the plaintiff $7 million.
As of the 2011–12 school year, [PERSON] had an enrollment of 5,398 students at its three main campuses and 1,317 children at its preschools, for a total enrollment of 5,416. Beyond its campuses, [PERSON] served an estimated 46,923 [NORP] in 2011 through its support for public schools, charter schools, and families and caregivers throughout [GPE].
According to the Kamehameha Schools home website, the mission statement is as follows: "Kamehameha Schools' mission is to fulfill [ORG]'s desire to create educational opportunities in perpetuity to improve the capability and well-being of people of [NORP] ancestry."
